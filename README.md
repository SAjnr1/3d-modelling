Engineering Logo


The  Engineering logo for the Presbyterian Boys' Secondary School (PRESEC) - Legon is a custom CAD design created in Onshape as part of my Hack Club Horizons journey. The goal of this project was to transform the Engineering logo into a detailed three-dimensional model that can be 3D printed and used for exhibitions, awards, branding, and STEM outreach activities.

The badge incorporates several engineering symbols, including a gear, circuit traces, a wrench, a safety helmet, and a digital display, all of which represent different aspects of engineering and innovation.



Inspiration 

As a member of the PRESEC Engineering community, I wanted to create something that represents the spirit of engineering at my school. Rather than using a flat logo, I challenged myself to recreate it as a 3D model that could be physically manufactured and displayed.

This project allowed me to combine creativity, design thinking, and CAD skills while producing something meaningful for my school community.

What I designed and how I designed it: 
Using Onshape and a 2D image of the original logo as a reference, I made the design as a detailed 3D model by:
1. Raised PRESEC Engineering lettering
2. A central gear representing mechanical engineering
3. Circuit pathways representing electronics and technology
4. A wrench representing practical engineering
5. A safety helmet symbolizing professionalism and safety
6. A digital display element representing innovation and design

The final model is suitable for 3D printing and can be scaled for different applications such as plaques, keychains, awards, or display pieces.
